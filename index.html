Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the south bank of the Yamuna river in the Indian city of Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a minaret that forms part of the Qutub complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Mehrauli area of Delhi, India. It is made of red sandstone and marble and stands at a height of 73 meters.

Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal, or "Palace of Winds," is a palace in Jaipur, India. Built from red and pink sandstone, it was designed in 1799 by Lal Chand Ustad in the form of the crown of Krishna, the Hindu god.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is a historic fort in the city of Delhi, India. It served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors for nearly 200 years, until 1857. It was built by Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century.
